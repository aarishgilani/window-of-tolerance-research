# Lesson 11 — The Regulation Stack and Practice Ecology

**Source type:** Course lesson note
**Date ingested:** 2026-06-23

## Summary

- Synthesizes the entire course into a functional **practice ecology**: categorizing existing routines by their primary physiological mechanism rather than their traditional labels.
- Introduces the [[regulation-stack]] — a three-tiered strategy (Acute Resets / Daily Maintenance / Structural Builders) with strict sequencing logic: never attempt Tier 3 work on a Tier 1 budget.
- Deepens [[shadow-work]] with the "Golden Rule of Integration" (re-experiencing is not integrating), Dual Awareness (split-screen focus), and the Catharsis vs. Integration distinction.
- Introduces the [[training-recovery-curve]] — the "V" Curve of vagal withdrawal and parasympathetic rebound, HRV as a fuel gauge for deciding which tier to operate at, intra-workout regulation, and the Savasana Rule.
- Frames neuroscience knowledge itself as a regulatory tool: understanding mechanism recruits the PFC for top-down modulation, turning "bad" practice sessions into data-driven observations.
- Introduces the concept of a personalized [[nervous-system-field-guide]]: mapping individual "flickers," environmental anchors, and moral alignment as metabolic savings.

## Key Claims

1. Practices should be categorized by physiological mechanism — **Sensors** (interoception builders), **Hardware** (vagal tone trainers), **Capacity** (window expanders), **Clean-up Crew** (arousal cyclers) — rather than by traditional labels like "yoga" or "meditation." (Lesson 11 note)
2. **Gap Analysis**: lopsided practice ecologies (e.g., "all-sensors, no-brakes" or "mobilization addict") produce diminishing returns; the four functional categories must be balanced. (Lesson 11 note)
3. The [[regulation-stack]] uses strict sequencing: Tier 1 (Acute Resets) → Tier 2 (Daily Maintenance) → Tier 3 (Structural Builders). Effective integration requires a stable physiological platform funded by Tier 2 consistency. (Lesson 11 note)
4. **The Golden Rule of Integration**: meaning-making and narrative updates only happen inside the [[window-of-tolerance]]; outside the window is just re-traumatization. Re-experiencing is not integrating. (Lesson 11 note)
5. **Dual Awareness**: successful shadow work requires a "split-screen" focus (50% on the internal story, 50% on current physiology) to prevent flooding and keep the [[insular-cortex|Anterior Insula]] grounded in the present. (Lesson 11 note)
6. **Catharsis vs. Integration**: massive emotional "venting" outside the window often just practices dysregulation; true integration is staying regulated while high-charge energy completes its cycle. (Lesson 11 note)
7. The **"V" Curve**: training involves Vagal Withdrawal (effort) followed by Parasympathetic Rebound (recovery). Adaptation happens only if the "V" is allowed to complete. (Lesson 11 note)
8. **HRV as Fuel Gauge**: use RMSSD data to decide between Tier 3 expansion (High HRV), Tier 2 maintenance (Moderate HRV), or Tier 1 recovery (Low HRV). (Lesson 11 note)
9. **The Savasana Rule**: dedicate 5-10 minutes post-training to "closing the loop" — rapidly shifting to a high-vagal state turns on the "anabolic switch" for repair and prevents post-workout crashes. (Lesson 11 note)
10. **Analytical Grounding**: when facing dissociation (Dorsal shutdown), shifting focus to raw interoceptive data (heart rate, temperature) increases cognitive load, forcing the system to "fund" the PFC and initiate a sympathetic thaw. (Lesson 11 note)
11. **Moral Alignment as Safety**: based on Kim and Kim (2025), being in alignment with values acts as "metabolic savings," freeing energy for higher cognition rather than wasting it on internal defense. (Lesson 11 note)

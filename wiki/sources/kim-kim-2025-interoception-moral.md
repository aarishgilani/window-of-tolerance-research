# Kim & Kim (2025) — Neural Processes Linking Interoception to Moral Preferences

**Full title**: Neural Processes Linking Interoception to Moral Preferences Aligned with Group Consensus  
**Authors**: JuYoung Kim and Hackjin Kim  
**Affiliation**: Laboratory of Social and Decision Neuroscience, Korea University, Seoul  
**Journal**: *Journal of Neuroscience* (Behavioral/Cognitive section)  
**Year**: 2025  
**Type**: Behavioral + neuroimaging (resting-state fMRI); two independent experiments  
**Ingested**: 2026-06-15

## Summary

- **Experiment 1**: Self-report interoceptive awareness (K-MAIA, 32-item Korean Multidimensional Assessment of Interoceptive Awareness) + moral dilemmas task (self-paced vignettes on Cognition.run server)
- **Experiment 2**: Interoceptive accuracy via **heartbeat counting task** (Schandry 1981; 6 trials, 25–50 s each; IAcc = 1 − |actual − reported heartbeats| / actual heartbeats) + fMRI (Siemens 3T, 304 volumes at TR=1.35s) + same moral task
- **Moral measure**: Group consensus calculated as ratio of participants choosing utilitarian option per scenario; individual moral similarity = percentage of choices matching the majority-preferred option

## Key Findings

1. **Both self-report interoceptive awareness and objective interoceptive accuracy predict moral alignment with group consensus** (significant positive correlation, Bonferroni-corrected p < 0.05 in both experiments)
2. **Neural mediation**: Fractional occupancies of resting-state brain states involving the **ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC)** and the **precuneus** mediated the interoception → moral alignment relationship
3. The relationship held across male and female participants; replicated across two independent experiments

## Theoretical Framing

The paper draws on **allostasis and predictive processing**: conforming to majority-approved moral preferences reduces prediction error and the metabolic cost of inferring others' expectations. Interoception, as the system that tracks the body's allostatic state, provides the grounding for these "internalized models of what the majority expects" — high interoceptive awareness means more accurate sensing of the allostatic cost of social misalignment.

> The moral intuition is literally felt in the body; interoceptive fidelity determines how strongly that felt signal influences choice.

## Neural Pathway Clarification

The mediation runs: **Interoceptive ability → resting-state vmPFC/precuneus activity → moral choice alignment**. The **insular cortex** is the primary substrate for interoceptive processing (and thus the upstream source of the signal), but the study's explicit mediators are vmPFC and precuneus — not the insula directly. This distinction matters: the insula is where internal body signals are integrated; the vmPFC/precuneus are where those signals are translated into social/moral valuation.

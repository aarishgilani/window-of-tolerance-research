## Why It Was Flagged

This is a **medical education editorial** about Goodhart's Law ("When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure") and Campbell's Law, applied to graduate medical education (GME) assessment and program evaluation. The paper discusses how ACGME resident surveys, USMLE Step 1 scores, publication metrics, and other assessment targets become corrupted when used as performance measures, and proposes mitigation strategies (criterion-referenced assessments, mastery learning, narrative approaches).

While the paper is from a medical journal, its content is entirely about **educational measurement theory and program evaluation methodology**. It does not address the autonomic nervous system, polyvagal theory, interoception, HRV, breathwork, somatic practices, or any neuroscience topic covered by this wiki.

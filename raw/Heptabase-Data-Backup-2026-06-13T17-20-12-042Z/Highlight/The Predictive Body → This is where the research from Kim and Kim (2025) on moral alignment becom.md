# Highlight

This is where the research from **Kim and Kim (2025)** on moral alignment becomes fascinating. If your brain predicts that you must align with a group consensus to be "safe" (a top-down social prediction), but your body is sending signals of "disgust" or "rejection" regarding the group's actions (bottom-up interoceptive data), the Insula becomes a site of intense prediction error. The "moral" dimension of our nervous system is essentially the struggle to resolve the error between our social safety predictions and our internal somatic reality.

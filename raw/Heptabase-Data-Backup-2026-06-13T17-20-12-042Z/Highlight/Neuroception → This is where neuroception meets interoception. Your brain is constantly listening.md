# Highlight

This is where neuroception meets **interoception**. Your brain is constantly listening to the "hum" of your internal organs—your heart rate, the tension in your gut, the quality of your breath. If your heart starts racing due to a shot of caffeine, your neuroception might "misinterpret" that internal signal as a sign of external danger, shifting your mood toward anxiety even if your environment is perfectly safe.

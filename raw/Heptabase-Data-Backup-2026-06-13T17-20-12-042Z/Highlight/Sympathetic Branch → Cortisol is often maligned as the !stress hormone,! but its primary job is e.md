# Highlight

**Cortisol** is often maligned as the "stress hormone," but its primary job is energy management. It increases blood sugar (glucose) by breaking down stores in the liver and inhibits "non-essential" functions like growth and reproduction to ensure that every available calorie is directed toward the muscles and brain. While adrenaline is the sprinter, cortisol is the marathon runner that keeps you mobilized for the long haul.

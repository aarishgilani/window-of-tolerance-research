# Highlight

Why is it that two people can sit in the exact same open-plan office, under the same flickering fluorescent lights and the same hum of conversation, and have two completely different internal experiences? One person—let’s call him the Dandelion—barely notices the noise. He is focused, steady, and seemingly unbothered by the environment. The other person—the Orchid—feels every flicker of the light like a physical tap on the shoulder. The background chatter isn't just noise; it’s a series of distinct data streams that are impossible to ignore. By 2:00 PM, the Orchid is reaching the upper edge of their Window of Tolerance, while the Dandelion is just getting into their flow.

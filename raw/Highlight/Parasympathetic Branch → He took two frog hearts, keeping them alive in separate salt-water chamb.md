# Highlight

He took two frog hearts, keeping them alive in separate salt-water chambers. He stimulated the vagus nerve of the first heart, causing it to slow down. He then took the liquid from that chamber and poured it into the second chamber. Even though the second heart had not been electrically stimulated, it immediately began to slow down as well. 

Loewi had discovered what he called *Vagusstoff*—vagus-stuff. This substance was later identified as Acetylcholine.

# Highlight

Imagine a gazelle being chased by a cheetah. The gazelle’s SNS is maxed out; it is in a state of high-intensity mobilization. If the gazelle is caught but the cheetah is distracted, the gazelle doesn't just stand up and walk away. It goes through a very specific, involuntary sequence. It will lie on the ground and begin to shake, tremor, and take deep, convulsive breaths. This process can last for several minutes. Once the shaking stops, the gazelle stands up, shakes its whole body one last time, and returns to grazing as if nothing happened.

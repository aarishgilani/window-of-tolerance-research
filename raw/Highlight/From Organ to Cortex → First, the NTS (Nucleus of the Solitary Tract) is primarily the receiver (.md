# Note

First, the **NTS (Nucleus of the Solitary Tract)** is primarily the **receiver** (the "ear"), not the trigger. The initial "knot" in your stomach is usually triggered by **top-down** signals from higher centers like the **Hypothalamus** or **Amygdala**. When you touch a difficult memory in shadow work, your brain performs "Neuroception" (detecting a threat). It sends an **efferent** (downward) command through the Sympathetic Nervous System or the Vagus nerve to physically constrict the gut or change its motility.

Here is where the loop you're describing kicks in:

1. **The "Knot" forms:** Higher brain centers order the physical "clench."

2. **The Vagus reports back:** The **afferent** (upward) fibers of the Vagus nerve detect this physical "clench" in the gut wall and send that data immediately to the **NTS**. 

3. **The NTS confirms the threat:** The NTS receives this "clench" signal and passes the data to the Thalamus and Hypothalamus. It essentially tells the brain: *"The weather report confirms a storm; the gut is indeed tight."*

4. **Sympathetic Dominance:** Because the NTS has confirmed the "storm," the brain (Hypothalamus) decides to double down. It increases **Sympathetic dominance** (pumping more adrenaline/norepinephrine) because the body is reporting that it is already in a state of threat.

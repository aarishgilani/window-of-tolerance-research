# Hedging Against the “Dandelion Blindspots”

One of Taleb’s biggest warnings is against the danger of the **Narrative Fallacy**—the tendency to look at the past and assume the future will look exactly the same.

A society made entirely of Dandelions is highly susceptible to this blindspot. Because Dandelions thrive easily in standard conditions, they are prone to complacency. They assume that because a drought hasn't happened in 100 years, it won't happen tomorrow.

> **The Synthesis:** A purely Dandelion society is a sitting duck for a Black Swan. It functions perfectly right up until the moment it suddenly doesn't. The Orchids act as the cognitive diversity necessary to hedge against this blindness. They disrupt the consensus by saying, *"Hey, something feels wrong here,"* forcing the robust Dandelions to prepare.

### Summary Table

| **Concept** | **The Dandelion** | **The Orchid** |

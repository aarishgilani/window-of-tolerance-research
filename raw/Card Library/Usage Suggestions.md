# Usage Suggestions

+ How to organize fragmented ideas without a clear topic?

   [https://youtu.be/\_EeUvpVkxU8](https://youtu.be/_EeUvpVkxU8)

   1. If you have scattered thoughts but no clear topic yet, record them in your Journal.

   2. Later, if you want to review your past journals, right-click on a whiteboard, select *Calendar*, and pull all journal cards from a time range onto the whiteboard at once.

+ How to organize when you have many whiteboards and cards?

   [https://youtu.be/knxJ3Sw4gpw](https://youtu.be/knxJ3Sw4gpw)

   We recommend using a “two-level whiteboard structure” to organize knowledge and ideas:

   - **Parent whiteboard**: Projects, topics, life, books, courses, thought leaders, etc.

   - **Sub-whiteboards**: For each parent whiteboard, create sub-whiteboards for different project names, research topics, or life items.

+ Are there more real-world use cases of how others use Heptabase? 

   In the [Heptabase Gallery](https://heptabase.com/gallery), We curate and showcase many whiteboards from our users, where you can see how people use Heptabase for learning, research, and planning.

   When you’ve created a whiteboard you like, you’re welcome to share it through [this link](https://form.typeform.com/to/uNKKn2Gq) at any time. After it passes review, your whiteboard will be published so people can discover and learn from it!

+ When to use tags vs. whiteboards?

   Our official suggestion: Use whiteboards for *knowledge and idea* cards (e.g., book notes, research notes). Only consider using tags when you have a large number of *pure record/archive* cards (e.g., meeting notes, literature). Since Heptabase is designed for learning and researching complex topics, in 95% of cases we recommend using whiteboards.
